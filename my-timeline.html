  • 1763

  • The Royal Proclamation Treaty

  • The basis for the relationship between Federal, Provincial and aboriginal groups. One of the first legal documents that recognize the Indegenous peoples and their title to land rights.

    The formation of the treaty occured after the end of the Seven Years War (1756 - 1763), where Great Britain claimed victory over France, and was now ruling over land that would form both Canada and the United States. Altough this came before the establishment of Canada as a country, this treaty was responsible for the insitutional relationship between the government and aborigional groups that is still in effect today.
